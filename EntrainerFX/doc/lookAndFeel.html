Entrainer has a dynamic Swing look and feel architecture, as demonstrated in the previous pages. This is in addition to the ability to change the JavaFX controls via CSS. The default look and feel is 'Substance Creme', and can be changed to the user's preference.

The 'Napkin' Look and Feel

Changing the Look and Feel of Entrainer

Many look and feels are now included with the distribution, and more can easily be added. The Javootoo site contains links to many other look and feels. Simply download the look and feel jar, copy to the 'lafs' directory of the Entrainer install and restart the application to load the new look and feels.

The exception is skinnable look and feels requiring themepacks, however the most versatile, Skin Look and Feel, is included with the distribution. To use a new skin download a themepack, save to the 'lafs/skins' directory of the Entrainer install and restart the application.

The following look and feels are included with the Entrainer distribution:

  1. Substance
  2. Napkin
  3. Skin Look and Feel
  4. Kunststoff
  5. JTattoo


Insubstantial - Substance Maintenance Fork

The Substance look and feel has been parked by its author, however it is being maintained in the Insubstantial fork. Starting with EntrainerFX 4.2.0 Final the latest insubstantial release (7.2.1) is included in the EntrainerFX distribution. To upgrade this look and feel the following jar files must be downloaded from the above site and placed in the 'lafs' directory:

  1. The substance jar file (substance-[release #].jar)
  2. The laf plugin jar file (laf-plugin-[release #].jar)
  3. The laf widget jar file (laf-widget-[release #].jar)

The old Substance jar files included with Entrainer should be renamed/moved from the 'lafs' directory as the differing versions of substance do not play well together when loaded dynamically. The latest versions of Substance are quite clean and professional and load significantly faster than the version included with Entrainer.

Notes

The overwhelming majority of the look and feels included with the Entrainer distribution will work as expected, however some look and feels are platform-specific, and some do not dynamically load properly. This may become evident with user-installed look and feels. The former is a design choice, the latter is a bug with the specific look and feel implementation. Neither is an issue with Entrainer.
